# Domain Maps

Curated vocabulary references that help you prompt AI tools with more specificity.

**Tagline:** Giving you just enough categories to help you prompt better.

## The Problem

The biggest bottleneck to getting good AI output isn't the AI — it's that people don't know the terminology of the domain they're working in. If you don't know what a "combobox" is, you can't ask for one. If you don't know what "cove lighting" means, you'll just say "make it cozy."

## The Solution

Domain Maps provides curated vocabulary for fields people are curious about but typically lack formal training in:

- **Content Creation** — Video production and writing
- **Interior Design** — Spaces, materials, and atmosphere
- **Fashion Design** — Clothing, textiles, and styling
- **Cooking** — Technique, flavor, and composition

Each domain is structured as: **Domains → Branches → Categories → Terms**

Deliberately not exhaustive. Think 10 terms per category — enough to go from vague to specific, not so many it becomes a textbook.

## Design

Minimal, archival, almost academic — inspired by are.na, dictionary indexes, and ontology/taxonomy references. Quiet, typographic, lots of whitespace. Not flashy UI. It should feel like a tool a design researcher would bookmark.

## Usage

Open `index.html` in your browser. Click any term to copy it to your clipboard.
